Lemma 11.4:
Let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence of holomorphic functions on a domain  $\Omega$ and assume there exists  $M < \infty$ syuch that
\[
|f_n(z)| \le M \qquad \forall z \in \Omega, \forall n
\] 
then $\{f_n\}$ has a convergent subsequence $\{f_{n_j}\}$. that converges uniformly on every compact $K \subset \Omega$.\\
Let $A$ be the  set of all dyadic points in $ \mathbb{C}$ then $A \cap \Omega = \{q_1,q_2,\dots,\}$ is countable. 
Create a sequence $f_{1n}$ such that  $\lim_{n\to \infty}f_{1,n}(q_1) \to f(q_1)$ and from this sequence we take a subsequence $f_{2,n}$ converges at $q_2$. then $f_{n,n}$ converges to  $f(q_i)$ for all  $i \in \mathbb{N}$.
Also $\{f_n\}$ is equicontinuous in  $K$ for all $K \subset \Omega$ compact.\\
Equicontinuous, $\forall \epsilon > 0, \forall K \subset \Omega$ compact there exists $\delta$ such that $z,w \in K$ and  $|z-w| < \delta$ implies
\[
\sup_n |f_n(z) - f_n(w)| < \epsilon
\] 
Let $\eta_k = d(K, \partial \Omega) > 0$ and $z \in K$  $\omega \in \Omega$ and  $|z-w| < \frac{1}{2}\eta_k$.
Then
\[
|f_n(z) - f_n(\omega)| = | \int_{ z}^{w}f_n'(\zeta) d\zeta|
\] 
but
\[
|f'(z)) |  \le \frac{M}{d(z, \partial \Omega)} \le \frac{2}{\eta_k}M
\] 
so
\[
|f_n(z) - f_n(\omega)| \le \frac{2M}{\eta}|z-\omega| 
\] 
so $f_n$ are equicontinuous on compact sets.
For all  $K \subset \Omega$ compact $\{f_{n,n}(z)\}$ is cauchy uniformly in  $z \in K$ Let  $\epsilon > 0$
there exists $N(\epsilon,K)$ such that $m,n \ge N$
 \[
     |f_{n,n}(z) - f_{m,m}(z)| < \epsilon
\] 
$K$ compact implies there exists $z_1,z_2,\dots,z_N \in K$ such that
\[
    K = \bigcup_{i=1}^{N}\{|z-z_i| < \delta\} = \bigcup_{i=1}^{N}B_i
\] 
there exists $p_i \in A \cap \Omega$ $p_i \in B_i \subset \Omega$. There exists $n_\epsilon$ such that $1 \le j \le M$
$n,m > n_\epsilon$ 
\[
 |f_{n,n}(p_j) -  f_{m,m}(p_j)| < \epsilon
\] 
$z \in K, z \in B(z,\delta_k)$ then
\[
 |f_{n,n}(z) - f_{m,m}(z)| \le |f_{n,n}(z) - f_{n,n}(p_j)| + |f_{n,n}(p_j) + f_{m,m}(p_j)| + |f_{m,m}(p_j) - f_{m,m}(z)| < 3\epsilon
\]
